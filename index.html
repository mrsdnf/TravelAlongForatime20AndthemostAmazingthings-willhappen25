When to Use Which Tier

The key question: Is this new and revolutionary? Features that give us competitive advantage and don't exist elsewhere in the industry should stay internal. For everything else, trusted external AI is fine.

Rule of thumb: If a competitor could build something similar with public knowledge, Tier 2 is fine. If it's truly novel and gives us an edge, keep it in Tier 1.

Why Chinese AI Is a Legal Risk

National Intelligence Law (2017)

Requires all Chinese companies to "support, assist and cooperate with state intelligence work." This means any data you input can be accessed by Chinese authorities.

Data Security Law (2021)

Gives the government broad authority to access data for "national security." Companies cannot refuse or even disclose that data was requested.

Bottom line: Anything you type into DeepSeek, Qwen, or Ernie could end up with the Chinese government. This includes design specs, user research, competitive strategies, and any text you input.

Feature Readiness

Use AI as your thinking partner

The Challenge

Turning initial ideas into well-defined features takes time. Important details can get missed and sometimes UX receives incomplete requirements and wastes time having to clarify.

The Solution

Use AI as a thinking partner to help structure your ideas. You bring the vision and business knowledge - AI helps you think it through more completely.

The Workflow

  1. Start - Share your rough concept with AI
  2. Explore - Let AI ask clarifying questions
  3. Review - Accept what fits, reject what doesn't
  4. Complete - Add your expertise and context
  5. Finalize - Create the ADO feature

Key principle: AI is a thinking partner, not a replacement. Every decision remains yours.

Example Conversation Starter

"I'm working on a feature idea: '[Your concept]'.

Help me think this through:

  • What questions should I be asking?
  • Help me draft a user story
  • What acceptance criteria might I be missing?
  • What technical considerations should I discuss?"

Feature Readiness Checklist

  • User Story - As a [user], I want [goal], so that [benefit]
  • Problem Statement - What pain point does this solve?
  • Acceptance Criteria - Testable conditions for "done"
  • Target Users - Which personas or segments?
  • Success Metrics - How do we measure success?
  • Scope Boundaries - What's out of scope?
  • Technical Constraints - API limits, dependencies?
  • Business Context - Priority and stakeholders

Initial Investigation

Find context and answers faster

The Challenge

"Who do I ask?" and "Can we technically do this?" - Finding the right people and technical constraints can slow down the design process.

The Solution

Use Ask Rovo in Confluence to quickly answer context questions about ownership, constraints, and history across all your connected tools.

What Rovo Can Do

  • Unified Search - Search Jira, Confluence & connected tools
  • Natural Language - Ask questions in plain English
  • Deep Research - Get cited reports across sources
  • Respects Permissions - Only shows what you can access
Example: Ask Rovo

"I am designing the 'Upload Invoice' flow:

  • What are the known API file size limits?
  • Who is the Tech Lead for the 'Documents' service?
  • Were there any previous attempts at this feature?"

Rovo searches across Jira, Confluence, and connected tools to find answers with citations.

Keeping Confluence Fresh

Rovo is only as good as your documentation. For Confluence to become the place where you find information instead of where it goes to die, content must stay current.

Automate Content Lifecycle

  • Auto-archive stale pages - Archive pages not viewed in 180 days or not updated in 365 days
  • Review reminders - Send weekly notifications to page owners before archiving
  • Flag for review - Automatically add "requires review" labels to aging content

Establish Ownership

  • Assign page owners - Every page needs someone responsible for its accuracy
  • Use review labels - Tag pages with "6 months" or "12 months" review cycles
  • Bulk cleanup - Use Content Manager to find and archive outdated pages

Pro tip: Confluence Automation (Premium) can handle this automatically. Without Premium, apps like Workflows for Confluence or Breeze provide similar capabilities.

  • User Interviews - Conduct and analyze 1:1 sessions

"I'm attaching summaries from 6 user interviews. Identify: (1) The top 5 recurring pain points with frequency, (2) Any surprising outliers, (3) Quotes that best illustrate each pain point."

UI Flows & Edge Cases

Think through the "rainy day" scenarios

The Challenge

Mapping out error cases, edge cases, and "rainy day" scenarios is time-consuming but critical. It's easy to miss important states.

The Solution

Use AI as a "pessimist" to help identify edge cases and error states you might have missed. Generate the logic flow before drawing arrows.

Pro Tip: Mermaid.js

Ask Chat GNPT to output flowcharts in Mermaid.js syntax, then paste into:

  • Draw.io / Diagrams.net
  • Miro
  • Notion
  • GitHub markdown
Example: Edge Case Discovery

"I am designing the 'Password Reset' flow. The happy path is successful reset.

  • List 10 edge cases and error states I should consider
  • Include things like: weak password, no network, expired link, server timeout
  • Output as a text-based flow diagram in Mermaid.js syntax"

When to Use / Not Use AI

Know where AI helps and where it doesn't

AI is powerful for structured, repetitive, and exploratory tasks. It struggles with empathy, novelty, and precision. Use this guide to decide when to reach for AI and when to rely on your expertise.

When to Use AI

  • Thinking partner - Structure ideas and find gaps
  • Ideation - Generate layout variations
  • Repetitive tasks - Rename layers, fill mock data
  • Edge cases - Find error states you missed
  • Documentation - Format decisions and test cases
  • Context lookup - Find owners and history

When NOT to Use AI

  • User empathy - AI hallucinates emotions
  • Visual polish - Humans must pixel-peep
  • Novel interactions - AI gives generic patterns
  • Technical facts - Always verify with devs
  • Final decisions - You own the outcome
  • Confidential data - Use internal tools only
